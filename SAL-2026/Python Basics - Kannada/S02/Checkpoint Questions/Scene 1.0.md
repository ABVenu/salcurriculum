#### **Scene 1.0**

1. **What is an operator in Python?**  
   a) A variable name used to store text values  
   b) A special symbol that performs an operation on one or more values (operands)  
   c) A comment line starting with the `#` symbol  
   d) A function that displays output on the screen  

   **Answer:** b) A special symbol that performs an operation on one or more values (operands)  

   **Explanation:** Operators are symbols like `+`, `*`, and `/` that tell Python to calculate, compare, or combine values — turning stored data into useful results.

2. **What is the result of `17 // 5` in Python?**  
   a) `3.4`  
   b) `3`  
   c) `2`  
   d) `12`  

   **Answer:** b) `3`  

   **Explanation:** Floor division (`//`) divides two numbers and keeps only the whole-number quotient — 5 fits into 17 exactly three times, so the answer is `3`.

3. **What is the result of `17 % 5` in Python?**  
   a) `3`  
   b) `3.4`  
   c) `2`  
   d) `0`  

   **Answer:** c) `2`  

   **Explanation:** The modulus operator (`%`) gives the remainder after division — 17 ÷ 5 leaves a remainder of `2`.

4. **What is the result of `5 ** 2` in Python?**  
   a) `10`  
   b) `25`  
   c) `7`  
   d) `52`  

   **Answer:** b) `25`  

   **Explanation:** The `**` operator performs exponentiation — `5 ** 2` means 5 raised to the power of 2 (5 × 5), which equals `25`. It is used for squaring sides when calculating area.

5. **A customer buys 3 kg of rice at ₹55/kg and 1 kg of dal at ₹90/kg. What is the total bill?**  
   a) `₹165`  
   b) `₹255`  
   c) `₹345`  
   d) `₹90`  

   **Answer:** b) `₹255`  

   **Explanation:** Rice cost is `3 × 55 = 165`, dal cost is `1 × 90 = 90`, and adding both gives `165 + 90 = 255`.
